VAST MC 2020

To identify the hackers, the task at hand involves examining CGCS records to pinpoint groups that closely match a profile identified by CGCS sociopsychologists. The researchers at the Center for Global Cyber Strategy (CGCS) have utilized data from white hat groups to create anonymized profiles of various groups. Among these profiles, some have been singled out as closely resembling the structure of the group responsible for the recent internet outage. The objective is to analyze CGCS records and identify groups that best fit the identified profile, contributing to the identification of the hackers behind the recent internet outage.

Visualizations and Legends:

Stream Graph:

Legend: Colors represent different weeks, and the height of each layer indicates the frequency of communication during that week.

Pie Chart:

Legend: Each segment represents a different edge type, showcasing the diversity of communication channels within the group.

Heat Map:

Legend: Colors indicate the frequency of communication on each day, providing a temporal breakdown of the group's activity.

World Map:

Legend: Points on the map represent meet-up locations, helping to identify geographical patterns in the group's interactions.

Bar Chart:

Legend: Bars display demographic data, providing a visual representation of key characteristics within the identified profile.

Network Graph:

Legend: Nodes represent individuals, and the connections between them illustrate the group's social structure. Node colors reflect different characteristics.

Donut Chart:

Legend: Each ring in the donut chart represents demographic data for individual nodes, offering a detailed breakdown of group members' characteristics.

By analyzing these visualizations collectively, you can uncover patterns and similarities that will guide you in identifying the group that aligns most closely with the profile associated with the recent internet outage.
